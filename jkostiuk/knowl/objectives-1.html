Learning Outcomes 1.1.1.
By the end of this course, you will be able to:
  1. hold a conversation with an expert about linear algebra concepts using correct terminology;
  2. use the critical and abstract thinking skills developed in this course as a foundation for future courses in geometry and algebra;
  3. work productively with a team to analyze different approaches to solving the same problem;
  4. use introspective methods to help you monitor the development of your mathematical maturity;
  5. produce accurate, well-written arguments that communicate solutions to problems that are audience-appropriate;
  6. use your knowledge of algorithms in linear algebra to solve systems of linear equations and to calculate optimal coordinate systems for linear transformations;
  7. explain phenomena surrounding the nature of systems of linear equations, and properties of transformations, in both geometric and algebraic terms;
  8. apply your linear algebra skills to solve problems from a variety of other disciplines.
